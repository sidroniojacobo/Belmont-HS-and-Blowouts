Methodology

Sources

This project compiles various primary and secondary sources, such as oral histories, newspapers, archives, school governing boards, that reference Belmont High School. At the center, this project utilizes personal narratives of key actors who recall being involved at efforts at Belmont High School. These personal narratives are secondary sources given that the oral histories are housed in the UCLA Oral Histories Archive. This public archive has 14 separate oral histories of Chicanx leaders who were “were selected based on their experience as members or leaders of Chicana and Chicano Movement organizations from 1962 to 1978” (UCLA Oral History). In particular, I mainly draw on the oral histories of Joe Razo, David Sanchez, and Victoria Castro, two whom were student leaders during the late 1960’s. I also heavily rely on the Chicano Studies Research Library at the University of California - Chicano Newspaper Collection and Sal Castro Papers Collection. This is because Sal Castro, a teacher at Lincoln High School, is noted as a key leader in the Chicano Blowouts of 1968 due to his leadership role as an educator enabling students to protest and march at Lincoln High School but he also worked at Belmont High School (Muñoz 1989). I also relied on digital/physical yearbooks and digital/physical newspapers.
