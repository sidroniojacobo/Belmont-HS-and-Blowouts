At the center, this project utilizes personal narratives of key actors who recall being involved at efforts at Belmont High School. These personal narratives are secondary sources given that the oral histories are housed in the UCLA Oral Histories Archive. This public archive has 14 separate oral histories of Chicanx leaders who were “were selected based on their experience as members or leaders of Chicana and Chicano Movement organizations from 1962 to 1978” (UCLA Oral History). All oral histories are about 2-4 hour longs and they were recorded in several sessions. In order to analyze these oral histories as texts, I downloaded the oral histories and converted them to microsoft word documents. Then I uploaded all fourteen oral histories into Voyant to analyze them together. I decided to use the "Words in Context" feature of Voyant, to be able to analyze all the times Belmont High School was referenced or mentioned. I also did this for the other schools that participated in the walkouts: Roosevelt, Wilson, Franklin, Lincoln, and Garfield. As I had suspected, Belmont was referenced the least by participants, and it was mentioned the least out of all high schools. I did the same for the book "Blowout: Sal Castro and the Fight for Educational Justice", which is a biography of Sal Castro written by Miguel Castro. Sal Castro, a teacher at Lincoln High School, is noted as a key leader in the Chicano Blowouts of 1968 due to his leadership role as an educator enabling students to protest and march at Lincoln High School but he also worked at Belmont High School (Muñoz 1989). When I used the "Words in Context" feature to understand how the high schools were referenced, it was different from the Oral History Project. Sal Castro mentioned reference Belmont at higher rates. To see my analysis, please refer below. In addition to understand the frequency of participants mentioning Belmont, I was able to analyze the contexts. I downloaded both of the files containing the references of "Belmont" to provide the stories relating to Belmont High School and the Walkouts of 1968.
